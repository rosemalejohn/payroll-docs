# [GW] Payroll - Payslip Computation Engine

## Description

Implement the full gross-to-net payroll computation pipeline (ComputePayslipAction) and the batch recomputation action (RecomputePayrollRunAction). The pipeline processes each employee's payslip through a strict 10-step sequence: gross pay calculation, bonus application, absent deduction, SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG statutory deductions, HMO, voluntary deductions, cash advance recovery, BIR withholding tax, and net pay. Employees with external salary skip steps 3-9. The batch recomputation re-runs the full pipeline for every payslip in a draft payroll run within a single transaction.

## Technical Implementation

### ComputePayslipAction — 10-Step Pipeline

#### Step 1: Gross Pay Calculation

- Loads employee compensation data and checks the external salary flag.
- Loads the currency snapshot rate for the employee's salary currency.
- Calculates gross pay based on salary basis:
  - Monthly: monthly salary directly.
  - Daily: daily rate multiplied by working days.
- Converts to PHP using the snapshot rate.
- Snapshots salary fields on the payslip (monthly salary, daily rate, currency ID, currency code, exchange rate).
- Records a BASIC earning line item with the computed gross pay amount.

#### Step 2: Bonus Application

- Iterates over payroll run bonuses. For each bonus, checks if the employee is excluded via the bonus exclusions table.
- Computes the bonus amount based on computation type:
  - Fixed: flat amount from the payroll run bonus record.
  - Percentage: basis multiplied by rate divided by 100 (basis is gross pay or basic salary depending on configuration).
  - Formula (13th Month Pay): annual basic divided by 12 (monthly: monthly salary × 12 / 12; daily: daily rate × 261 / 12).
  - Formula (June Special): 100% of gross pay.
  - Attendance: checks attended days (working days minus absent days) against a threshold; awards the fixed amount if met.
- Records a bonus-type line item per applied bonus. Skips zero-amount bonuses (no line item created).
- Updates payslip total bonuses with the sum of all applied bonus amounts.

#### Step 3: Absent Deduction (skipped for external salary)

- Calculates a daily rate for deduction: monthly salary divided by 22 for monthly-basis, or daily rate directly for daily-basis.
- Multiplies by absent days.
- Records an ABSENT deduction line item.

#### Step 4: SSS Contribution (skipped for external salary)

- Uses a bracket-based lookup table stored in the payroll config to find the employee share based on Step 1 gross pay.
- Records an SSS deduction line item.

#### Step 5: PhilHealth Contribution (skipped for external salary)

- Applies 2.5% employee share with a salary floor of 10,000 and ceiling of 100,000.
- Uses Step 1 gross pay as basis.
- Records a PHILHEALTH deduction line item.

#### Step 6: Pag-IBIG Contribution (skipped for external salary)

- Fixed amount of PHP 100.00 employee share.
- Records a PAGIBIG deduction line item.

#### Step 7: HMO Deduction (skipped for external salary)

- Uses the fixed HMO amount stored on the employee record.
- If the amount is zero or null, no line item is created.

#### Step 8: Voluntary Deductions (skipped for external salary)

- MP2 (Modified Pag-IBIG 2): applied only when the employee's MP2 flag is enabled. Uses the MP2 amount from employee compensation.
- SSS WISP (Workers' Investment and Savings Program): applied only when the employee's SSS WISP flag is enabled. Uses the SSS WISP amount from employee compensation.
- Cash Advance Recovery: sums the remaining balance from all active cash advances for the employee. The actual balance deduction from cash advance records happens at finalization, not at computation time.
- Records deduction-type line items only for non-zero amounts.

#### Step 9: BIR Withholding Tax (skipped for external salary)

- Computes taxable monthly income as gross pay minus SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG deductions only. Bonuses, absent deductions, HMO, and voluntary deductions are NOT included in the taxable base.
- Annualizes the taxable monthly amount (multiplied by 12).
- Looks up the applicable BIR TRAIN Law tax bracket.
- Computes annual tax as base tax plus the excess over the bracket floor multiplied by the marginal rate.
- Divides annual tax by 12 and rounds to 2 decimal places for the monthly BIR withholding tax.
- Records a BIR_TAX deduction line item for non-zero amounts.
- Stores the BIR tax amount on the payslip record.

#### Step 10: Net Pay and External Salary Path

- Standard path (no external salary): sums all deductions (absent, SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, HMO, MP2, SSS WISP, cash advance, BIR tax), computes net pay as gross pay plus total bonuses minus total deductions, floors net pay at zero, and sets a net pay floored flag if the floor is triggered.
- External salary path: Steps 3-9 are skipped entirely. Total deductions is zero. Net pay equals gross pay plus total bonuses. All individual deduction fields are set to zero.
- For all employees: updates all computed fields on the payslip including gross pay, total bonuses, total deductions, net pay, and computed timestamp.

### RecomputePayrollRunAction — Batch Recompute

- Validates the run is in draft state (rejects non-draft runs with an error).
- Deletes all existing line items for all payslips in the run using a bulk query.
- Resets all computed fields on each payslip to zero.
- Re-runs the compute payslip action for each payslip.
- Recalculates run-level totals (total gross, total deductions, total net, total bonuses, employee count) using aggregate SQL queries.
- The entire operation runs inside a database transaction for atomicity — if any single payslip computation fails, the entire operation rolls back.
- This action is triggered automatically when data affecting computation changes (absent days, bonuses, employees, compensation).

### Environment & Config

- SSS contribution table stored in the payroll config file as an array of brackets with range_from, range_to, and employee_share values. Approximately 30 brackets following the current SSS schedule.
- BIR TRAIN Law tax brackets stored in the payroll config file as an array of brackets with min, max, base_tax, rate, and excess_over values. Six brackets from 0% (up to 250,000 annual) to 35% (above 8,000,000 annual).

### Edge Cases & Notes

- If the employee has no compensation record, the action must fail with a clear error.
- If the currency snapshot is not found for a foreign currency employee, the action must fail with a clear error.
- Zero monthly salary or daily rate results in gross pay of 0, but the BASIC line item is still created.
- All values must be stored using DECIMAL types with appropriate precision — no float arithmetic.
- Bonus with zero amount or rate does not generate a line item.
- Employee excluded from all bonuses results in total bonuses of 0.
- No bonuses on the run means Step 2 completes with total bonuses of 0.
- 13th Month Pay uses 261 standard working days per year for daily-basis employees.
- The divisor of 22 represents standard working days per month for monthly-basis employees.
- Zero absent days results in zero absent deduction.
- Gross pay below the SSS minimum bracket returns the minimum employee share.
- Gross pay above the SSS maximum bracket returns the maximum employee share.
- Gross pay below PhilHealth floor is floored to 10,000; above the ceiling is capped at 100,000.
- Employee with HMO amount of zero: Step 7 is skipped, no line item.
- Employee with MP2 flag disabled: MP2 is skipped regardless of MP2 amount.
- Employee with SSS WISP flag disabled: SSS WISP is skipped regardless of amount.
- Employee with no active cash advances: cash advance total is zero, no line item.
- Employee with multiple active cash advances: all balances are summed into a single deduction.
- Taxable annual income at or below 250,000 results in zero tax (first bracket).
- Negative taxable monthly (unlikely but guarded) is treated as zero taxable, resulting in zero tax.
- Very high salaries (above 8 million annual) fall into the 35% top bracket.
- Monthly BIR tax is rounded to 2 decimal places.
- Deductions exceeding gross plus bonuses result in net pay floored to zero with the floored flag set to true.
- External salary with no bonuses: net pay equals gross pay.
- External salary with bonuses: net pay equals gross pay plus total bonuses.
- Zero gross pay: net pay equals total bonuses minus total deductions (floored at zero).
- For large runs (500+ employees), consider chunking the payslip iteration for performance.
- Line item deletion uses a bulk query rather than iterating per payslip.
- Run-level totals use aggregate SQL queries rather than PHP-side summation.
