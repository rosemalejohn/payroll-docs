# [GW] Payroll - ComputePayslipAction - BIR Withholding Tax (Step 9)

## Description

Implement Step 9 of the 10-step payroll computation pipeline: BIR withholding tax calculation using the TRAIN Law (Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion) annual tax brackets. The taxable base is gross pay minus statutory deductions only (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG). The monthly income is annualized, the tax bracket is applied, and the annual tax is divided by 12 to yield the monthly withholding tax. This step is skipped for employees with external salary enabled.

## Technical Implementation

### What Actions

- ComputePayslipAction (Step 9 segment): skipped if the employee has external salary enabled. Computes taxable monthly income as gross pay minus SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG deductions. Annualizes the taxable monthly amount (multiplied by 12). Looks up the applicable BIR TRAIN Law tax bracket. Computes annual tax as base tax plus the excess over the bracket floor multiplied by the marginal rate. Divides annual tax by 12 and rounds to 2 decimal places for the monthly BIR withholding tax. Records a BIR_TAX deduction line item for non-zero amounts. Stores the BIR tax amount on the payslip record.

### Environment & Config

- BIR TRAIN Law tax brackets stored in the payroll config file as an array of brackets with min, max, base_tax, rate, and excess_over values. Six brackets from 0% (up to 250,000 annual) to 35% (above 8,000,000 annual).

### Tests

- Assert BIR withholding tax is computed from the correct taxable base: gross pay minus SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG.
- Assert bonuses, absent deductions, HMO, and voluntary deductions are NOT subtracted from the taxable base.
- Assert annualization is correct: taxable monthly multiplied by 12.
- Assert bracket lookup returns the proper base tax plus excess calculation for each tier.
- Assert employees with taxable annual income at or below 250,000 have zero BIR tax.
- Assert monthly BIR tax equals annual tax divided by 12, rounded to 2 decimal places.
- Assert a BIR_TAX deduction line item is created for non-zero tax amounts.
- Assert no line item is created when BIR tax is zero.
- Assert BIR tax amount is stored on the payslip record.
- Assert tax brackets are configurable and easily updatable.
- Assert Step 9 is skipped entirely for employees with external salary enabled.

### Edge Cases & Notes

- Taxable annual income at or below 250,000 results in zero tax (first bracket).
- Negative taxable monthly (unlikely but guarded) is treated as zero taxable, resulting in zero tax.
- Very high salaries (above 8 million annual) fall into the 35% top bracket.
- Monthly BIR tax is rounded to 2 decimal places.
