# [GW] Payroll - ComputePayslipAction - Absent and Statutory Deductions (Steps 3-6)

## Description

Implement Steps 3 through 6 of the 10-step payroll computation pipeline: absent deduction, SSS contribution, PhilHealth contribution, and Pag-IBIG contribution. All four steps use Step 1's gross pay (basic salary) as the computation basis — not the adjusted gross after bonuses. These steps are skipped entirely for employees with the external salary flag enabled.

## Technical Implementation

### What Actions

- ComputePayslipAction (Steps 3-6 segment): all steps are skipped if the employee has external salary enabled.
  - Step 3 (Absent Deduction): calculates a daily rate for deduction (monthly salary divided by 22 for monthly-basis, or daily rate directly for daily-basis), then multiplies by absent days. Records an ABSENT deduction line item.
  - Step 4 (SSS Contribution): uses a bracket-based lookup table stored in the payroll config to find the employee share based on Step 1 gross pay. Records an SSS deduction line item.
  - Step 5 (PhilHealth Contribution): applies 2.5% employee share with a salary floor of 10,000 and ceiling of 100,000. Records a PHILHEALTH deduction line item.
  - Step 6 (Pag-IBIG Contribution): fixed amount of PHP 100.00 employee share. Records a PAGIBIG deduction line item.
- Updates payslip with individual deduction amounts.

### Environment & Config

- SSS contribution table stored in the payroll config file as an array of brackets with range_from, range_to, and employee_share values. Approximately 30 brackets following the current SSS schedule.

### Tests

- Assert absent deduction uses monthly salary divided by 22 as daily rate for monthly-basis employees.
- Assert absent deduction uses daily rate directly for daily-basis employees.
- Assert absent deduction equals daily rate for deduction multiplied by absent days.
- Assert SSS bracket lookup returns the correct employee share for the given gross pay.
- Assert SSS uses Step 1 gross pay as basis, not adjusted gross.
- Assert PhilHealth applies 2.5% employee share with a floor of 10,000 and ceiling of 100,000.
- Assert PhilHealth uses Step 1 gross pay as basis.
- Assert Pag-IBIG deduction is always fixed at PHP 100.00.
- Assert all four deductions are recorded as deduction-type line items.
- Assert individual deduction amounts are stored on the payslip record.
- Assert Steps 3-6 are skipped entirely for employees with external salary enabled.

### Edge Cases & Notes

- The divisor of 22 represents standard working days per month for monthly-basis employees.
- Zero absent days results in zero absent deduction.
- Gross pay below the SSS minimum bracket returns the minimum employee share.
- Gross pay above the SSS maximum bracket returns the maximum employee share.
- Gross pay below PhilHealth floor is floored to 10,000; above the ceiling is capped at 100,000.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Server-side

#### Side-effects

- Absent deduction is calculated using monthly salary divided by 22 as the daily rate for monthly-basis employees, and the daily rate directly for daily-basis employees
- Absent deduction equals the daily rate for deduction multiplied by absent days
- SSS contribution is determined by a bracket-based lookup using the employee's gross pay
- SSS uses the Step 1 gross pay (basic salary) as the computation basis, not the adjusted gross after bonuses
- PhilHealth applies a 2.5% employee share with a salary floor of 10,000 and a ceiling of 100,000
- Pag-IBIG deduction is always fixed at PHP 100.00
- All four deductions are recorded as deduction-type line items on the payslip
- Individual deduction amounts are stored on the payslip record
- Steps 3 through 6 are skipped entirely for employees with the external salary flag enabled
- The SSS contribution table is configurable and easily updatable
